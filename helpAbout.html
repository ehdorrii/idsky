Scope

Many components of the Apollo spacecraft were connected to, and controlled by, the Apollo Guidance Computer (AGC): the gyroscope-based stable "platform" called the Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU), the accelerometers, the sextant and telescope, the radar systems, and the orientation and propulsion systems. Nevertheless, the most visible part of the Apollo computer was the Display and Keyboard (DSKY), since that was how the astronauts interacted with it. iDSKY affords you the opportunity to experience the unique Verb/Noun interface that was invented to allow the Apollo crews to efficiently and succinctly "talk" to the Apollo on-board computers.

Only a small subset of the functionality of the AGC is reproduced by iDSKY: its goal is to give a feel for the user interface of the historical Apollo Guidance Computer. While the basic operations (in particular the regular verbs) are faithfully simulated, most of the "real" functionality of the AGC — navigation and orientation control — is not available.

Exploring Further

The following resources were instrumental in the creation of iDSKY:

  • The Apollo Guidance Computer: Architecture and Operation, by Frank O'Brien. Springer Praxis, 2010.
  • Ron Burkey's Virtual AGC project on the web.
  • Building iPhone Apps with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, by Jonathan Stark. O'Reilly Media, 2010.

If you are interested in learning more about the Apollo Guidance Computer, the following will also be of interest:

  • Sunburst and Luminary: An Apollo Memoir, by Don Eyles. Fort Point Press, 2018.
  • Left Brains for the Right Stuff, by Hugh Blair-Smith. SDP Publishing, 2015.
  • Journey to the Moon: The History of the Apollo Guidance Computer, by Eldon Hall. AIAA, 1996.
  • Digital Apollo: Human and Machine in Spaceflight, by David Mindell. MIT Press, 2008.
